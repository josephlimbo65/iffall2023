About DIOR

Dior, short for Christian Dior, is a renowned French luxury fashion house that has left an indelible mark on the world of “haute couture” and ready-to-wear fashion. Founded by Christian Dior in 1946, the brand quickly rose to prominence for its timeless elegance, innovative designs, and impeccable craftsmanship. Christian Dior's debut collection in 1947, featuring the iconic "New Look," redefined post-war fashion with its voluminous skirts and nipped-in waists, setting the tone for a new era of femininity and glamour. Since then, Dior has continued to be a symbol of sophistication and luxury, offering a wide range of products, from clothing and accessories to fragrances and cosmetics. Dior's legacy endures as one of the most influential and coveted names in the global fashion industry.

1946: The Start of DIOR

With the financial backing of textile magnate Marcel Boussac, Christian Dior founded his eponymous fashion house in Paris. The brand quickly garnered attention, and its breakthrough moment came in 1947 when Dior unveiled his iconic "New Look" collection. This collection revolutionized post-war fashion with its emphasis on opulent, feminine silhouettes, marking the birth of a new era in haute couture.

1957: The Death of Christain Dior

Christian Dior's untimely death occurred on October 24, 1957, at the age of 52. His passing sent shockwaves through the fashion world, as he was at the height of his career and influence. Dior's death marked the end of an era but also ushered in a new chapter in the brand's history, as talented designers like Yves Saint Laurent and subsequent creative directors would continue to carry the Dior legacy forward, ensuring its enduring prominence in the world of haute couture and luxury fashion.

1960: The Transition

In 1960, the fashion world witnessed a significant transition at Dior as Yves Saint Laurent, Christian Dior's talented protege, assumed the creative reins of the brand's haute couture division. Saint Laurent's appointment marked a pivotal moment in Dior's history, as he began to infuse his own innovative and youthful design sensibilities into the brand. His early collections at Dior were characterized by a blend of tradition and modernity, laying the foundation for his own future success as a legendary fashion designer while continuing to contribute to the enduring legacy of the House of Dior.

2012: Raf Simons takes over

In 2012, a new era for Dior commenced when Raf Simons assumed the role of creative director. Simons brought a breath of fresh air to the brand, steering it towards a modern and minimalistic aesthetic. His tenure at Dior was marked by sleek silhouettes, innovative designs, and a departure from the ornate and dramatic styles of previous years. Simons' artistic vision breathed new life into the house, appealing to a younger generation while maintaining Dior's reputation for timeless elegance and sophistication.

2016: The first female creative director

In 2016, a historic milestone in the fashion world was achieved when Maria Grazia Chiuri was appointed as the first female creative director of Dior. Her arrival marked a significant moment in the brand's history as she introduced a feminist perspective to Dior's collections, emphasizing themes of female empowerment and gender equality. Chiuri's tenure has been characterized by designs that celebrate strength and individuality, making a bold statement about the evolving role of women in fashion and society, while continuing Dior's tradition of excellence and innovation.

Present: The legacy of Dior

Dior, the iconic French fashion house, continues to be a symbol of luxury, sophistication, and innovation. Known for its timeless elegance and commitment to craftsmanship, Dior offers a wide range of products, from haute couture and ready-to-wear fashion to accessories, fragrances, and cosmetics. With a history of influential creative directors, Dior has evolved to stay relevant in the ever-changing world of fashion, and it remains a dominant force in the global luxury industry, captivating fashion enthusiasts with its blend of tradition and modernity.
